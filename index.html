Career Journey & AI Exploration (1990 - 2026)

2023 - Present: A New Journey in AI & Longevity

  • 2023-Present: Founded Beargogo Media and established an intergenerational team to explore AI-assisted senior living models.
  • 2024-2026: Launched Beargogo Media on Amazon; published the picture book series A Silicon Baby Falls from the Sky and The Joy of Dining Alone.

Current Initiatives

  • Distributing content on “Human-AI Synergy” via Substack, YouTube, RedNote, and WeChat Channels.
  • Conducting user-side surveys on “Expectations and Concerns Regarding Robotic Companionship” to provide insights for research institutions and AI developers.

Future Vision

  • Collaborating on a 12-part video series, Joy in Solitary Living, in partnership with the Stanford Center on Longevity.
  • Organizing youth volunteer teams to work with embodied AI developers on pre-training AI literacy for potential users.

2019 - 2022: Post-Surgery Recovery & Community Outreach

  • 2019-2022: During cancer recovery, served as a volunteer lecturer at Silicon Valley senior centers, Cancer Society CA, and community workshops, promoting healthy cooking.
  • 2019-2022: Wrote cookbook Natural Diet for Diabetes and the recovery memoir The Gate of Rebirth.

2006 - 2018: Cross-disciplinary Publishing & Private Culinary Arts

  • 2006-2008: Editor-in-Chief of Diet and Health magazine, published by Cypress Book (US) Co.
  • 2008-2018: Served as a high-end private chef for a decade, specializing in customized healthy nutrition for Billionaire Families in Silicon Valley.
  • 2008-2018: Authored the narrative nonfiction Silicon Valley Ukiyoe: A Private Chef’s Ten-Year Memoir.
  • 2008-2018: Founded the lifestyle website www.beargogo.com, building an extensive online readership for culinary and aesthetic content.

1990 - 2005: Documentary & Media Production

  • 1990-1993: Visiting Scholar at San Francisco State University (SFSU) Cinema Department (granted U.S. residency via NIW Extraordinary Ability status).
  • 1993-1996: Editor-in-Chief of the Who’s Who of Chinese Americans, interviewing over 100 prominent figures in the Bay Area.
  • 1993-1996: Serialized the novel Earthquake and the philosophical column Golden Sands in Chinese-language newspapers across the U.S., Hong Kong, and Taiwan.
  • 1997-2005: Directed the children’s documentary series Rainbow Bridge (6 episodes) and the short-form series Interior Decoration in America (50 episodes), broadcast on Shanghai Oriental Television.
